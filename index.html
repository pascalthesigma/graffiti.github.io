What is the Graffiti Program?

The Graffiti Program is an initiative within Gorilla AG that functions similarly to finger painting but is specifically designed to support and promote smaller YouTubers and content creators who might not otherwise get the visibility they deserve.

We believe that creativity should be accessible to everyone, regardless of follower count or platform status. Our mission is to discover, nurture, and promote emerging talent in the digital content creation space.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I join the Graffiti Program?

To join the Graffiti Program, you need to submit an application through our website. We review applications on a quarterly basis and select creators based on their content quality, uniqueness, and potential for growth.

Is there a minimum follower requirement?

No, we don't have strict follower requirements. The Graffiti Program is specifically designed to support smaller creators with potential. We evaluate applications based on content quality and creator commitment rather than audience size.

Does it cost anything to join?

The Graffiti Program is completely free to join. We believe in removing barriers to entry for talented creators, not adding them.

What types of creators do you accept?

We welcome creators from all niches and platforms, including YouTube, Twitch, TikTok, and more. We value original content, consistency, passion, and a genuine desire to grow and contribute to the community.

Our Story

The Graffiti Program was born out of a vision to create a more inclusive and fair platform for content creators. When we noticed that Gorilla Tag's Finger Painter program felt too restrictive and seemed to overlook smaller creators, we decided to take action.

Two months ago, we established this program with a clear mission: to give deserving smaller creators the spotlight they merit. We believe that great content deserves recognition, regardless of follower count.

Our community-driven approach ensures that talent, creativity, and dedication are the primary factors in our selection process, creating opportunities for creators who might otherwise be overlooked.
